Taking Parents to Disneyland

Thinking of making a lasting memory with the folks before they croak? Then you are in the right place! This is the site that provides timeless information about taking your parents on a Disneyland vacation.

The following information will help you avoid common pitfalls and traps on your journey.

We believed in our idea - a family park where parents and children could have fun together. --Walt Disney1

Parents WILL Get Wet

A number of rides in the park have water as a main feature. Riders will get wet! This makes parents very nervous, but if you plan properly, you can make these watery rides enjoyable. Plan to get a fast pass for these rides so that you can come back in the sunny, hot afternoon and skip the long stand-by line. The warm sun will dry your clothes in no time. And if your parent really hates getting wet, bring a disposable poncho. They are cheap, lightweight, and packable.

For a day of magic and fun, follow this order:

  1. Eat an early breakfast.
  2. Head to the park 30 minutes before the gates open.
  3. Beat the lines and head to your favorite ride first.

And if you have time, fit some of these into your day:

  • Churros
  • Corn Dogs
  • Coffee . . . Lots of Coffee
